And now, monks, what is the sweetness of feelings?
Here, a monk, having abandoned desires and unwholesome states, attains and abides in the first Jhana, a state of joy and bliss arising from the abandonment of desires, with initial and sustained thought.
At that time, if he does not think of harming himself, does not think of harming others, does not think of harming both, at that time he feels a harmless feeling." 
"Now, monks, that supreme harmlessness, I say, is the sweetness of sensations."
