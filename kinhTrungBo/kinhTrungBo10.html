What are the four?
O monks, here a monk dwells contemplating the body on the body, ardently, mindfully, and with right mindfulness to overcome craving and sorrow in the world;
living contemplating feelings on feelings, ardently, mindfully, and with right mindfulness to overcome craving and sorrow in the world;
living contemplating the mind on the mind, ardently, mindfully, and with right mindfulness to overcome craving and sorrow in the world;
living contemplating phenomena on phenomena, ardently, mindfully, and with right mindfulness to overcome craving and sorrow in the world.

And, monks, what is it for a monk to contemplate the body on the body?
Here, monks, a monk goes to a forest, to the foot of a tree, or to an empty house, and sits cross-legged, with his back straight, and establishes mindfulness before him.
Aware, he breathes in;
Aware, he breathes out.
He breathes in deeply, and he understands:
"I breathe in deeply";
or he breathes out deeply, and he understands:
"I breathe out deeply";
He breathes in briefly, and he understands:
"I breathe in briefly";
He breathes out briefly, and he understands:
"I breathe out briefly."
"Feeling the whole body, I will breathe in," he practices;
"Feeling the whole body, I will breathe out," he practices;
"With a calm body, I will breathe in," he practices;
"With a calm body, I will breathe out," he practices.
Bhikkhus, just as a skilled windmill operator or his apprentice, when turning a long turn, understands:
"I am turning long" or when turning a short turn, understands:
"I am turning short."
Similarly, Bhikkhus, a monk who breathes in a long breath understands:
"I am breathing in long";
or breathes out a long breath, understands:
"I am breathing out long";
or breathes in a short breath, understands:
"I am breathing in short";
or breathes out a short breath, understands:
"I am breathing out short."
"With a feeling in the whole body, I will breathe in," he practices;
"With a feeling in the whole body, I will breathe out," he practices;
"With a calm body, I will breathe in," he practices;
"With a calm body, I will breathe out," he practices.
