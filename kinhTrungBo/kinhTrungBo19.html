"We live in contemplation and divide our thoughts into two."
Monks, whatever thoughts there are of desire, thoughts of anger, thoughts of harm, I divide into the first part;

With a mind that is calm, pure, untainted, free of afflictions, pliable, easy to use, solid, and calm, I guide and direct my mind to the knowledge of birth and death about sentient beings.
I, with pure supernatural eyes, see the life and death of living beings.
I understand that sentient beings, lowly, noble, beautiful, ugly, lucky, unfortunate, all depend on their karma.
Sentient beings do evil deeds of body, evil deeds of speech, evil deeds of mind, slander the Saints, follow wrong views, create karma according to wrong views.
These people, after the body breaks up and die, must be reborn in the evil realm, the evil realm, the lower realms, and hell.
As for those beings who achieve good deeds of body, achieve good deeds of speech, achieve good deeds of mind, do not slander the Saints, follow the right view, create karma according to the right view, these people, after the body breaks up and die, will be born in good destinations, the Heavens, in this world.

Monks, it is like in a dense forest there is a large, deep lake, and a large group of deer lives near one side.
There is a person who comes and doesn't want them to benefit, doesn't want them to be happy, and doesn't want them to be safe from troubles.
If there is a path that is peaceful, safe, leads to joy, he will block and close this path, will open dangerous paths, will place male prey, will place female prey.
Monks, like that, after a while that large group of deer met with trouble and gradually withered away.
Monks, there is a person coming who wants that group of deer to benefit, wants them to be happy, wants them to be safe from all misfortunes.
If there is a safe, secure path, he will open this path, close the dangerous path, take away the male prey, and destroy the female prey.
Monks, in this way, after a period of time, that large group of deer will grow, flourish, and become fulfilled.

Monks, those who want them to benefit, want them to be happy, want them to be at peace from all evils are only for the Tathagata, the Arahant, the Perfectly Enlightened One.
