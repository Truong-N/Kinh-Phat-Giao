At one time the World-Honored One was at Savatthi, at Jetavana, in the garden of Anathapindika (Anathapindika).
While staying at that place, the World-Honored One called the monks:
"Hey monks!" --"World-Honored One", those monks answered the World-Honored One.
The Ton said:
"Monks, I will teach you the method to protect against all defilements."
Listen and pay careful attention.
I will say."
--"Yes, Venerable One."
The World-Honored One said as follows:

-- Monks, I preach the destruction of defilements for those who know, for those who see, not for those who do not know, for those who do not see.
And this, monks, what is the eradication of defilements for others to know and for others to see:
There is wise intention and there is not wise intention.
Monks, due to improper attention, the taints that have not yet arisen arise, and the cankers that have already arisen increase.
Monks, due to wise attention, the taints that have not yet arisen do not arise, and the cankers that have arisen are eliminated.
Monks, there are cankers that must be eliminated by knowledge, there are cankers that must be eliminated by protection, there are cankers that must be eliminated by receiving, there are cankers that must be eliminated by patience, there are cankers that must be eliminated by avoidance, there are cankers that must be eliminated by eradication, there are cankers that must be eliminated by practice.

Monks, and what are the defilements that must be eliminated by knowledge?
Monks, here there are ordinary people who rarely hear, do not see the Saints, do not master the Dharma of the Saints, do not practice the Dharma of the Saints, do not see the Dharma of the Real Men, do not master the Dharma of the Real People, do not practice the Dharma of the Real People, do not understand the Dharma that requires attention, do not understand the Dharma that does not require attention;
Because he does not understand the dharmas that require attention, because he does not understand the dharmas that do not require attention, he pays attention to the dharmas that do not require attention and does not pay attention to the dharmas that require attention.

Monks, and what are the dharmas that do not require attention that he pays attention to?
Monks, it means that the dharma through his attention causes the cravings that have not yet arisen to arise, or the cravings that have already arisen to increase;
Either the unborn canker of existence arises, or the already arisen canker of existence increases, or the unborn canker of ignorance arises, or the already arisen canker of ignorance increases.
Those dharmas are dharmas that do not require attention, but he pays attention.
Monks, what are the dharmas that require attention that he does not pay attention to?
Monks, it means that through his mind the dharma of desire that has not yet arisen does not arise, or that the canker of desire that has arisen is eliminated, or that the canker of existence that has not yet arisen does not arise, or that the canker of existence that has already arisen is eliminated, or that the canker of ignorance that has not yet arisen does not arise, or that the canker of ignorance that has arisen is eliminated.
Those dharmas are dharmas that require attention but they do not pay attention.
Because he pays attention to dhammas that should not be noticed, because he does not pay attention to dhammas that need to be noticed, the cankers that have not yet arisen arise, and the cankers that have already arisen increase.

"I have a self", this wrong view arises to that person as real, as true;
"I have no self", this wrong view arises to him as real and true;

And monks, what are the defilements that must be eliminated by avoidance?
Monks, here, a monk who is wisely aware avoids evil elephants, avoids evil horses, avoids evil cows, avoids evil dogs, snakes, logs, thorns, deep pits, mountain abysses, dirty ponds, and puddles.
There are seats that are not worthy of sitting, residences that should not be visited, evil friends that if they associate with, they will be doubted and despised by wise fellow monks;
He, like the wise watcher, avoids that unworthy seat, stays in that place, and should not visit that place and those cruel friends.
Monks, if he does not avoid it like that, those destructive defilements and heat of mind may arise.
If he avoids it like that, those harmful defilements and heat will no longer exist.
Monks, these dharmas are called cankers that must be eliminated by avoidance.

And monks, what are the defilements that must be eliminated by elimination?
Monks, here, a monk who observes wisely does not accept the thoughts of desire that have arisen, abandons them, eliminates them, destroys them, does not allow them to exist;
not accepting the anger that has arisen, abandoning it, eliminating it, destroying it, not allowing it to exist (that anger);
not accepting harmful thoughts that have arisen, abandoning them, eliminating them, destroying them, not allowing them to exist (those harmful thoughts);
Do not accept the evil and unwholesome dharmas that always arise, abandon them, eliminate them, destroy them, and do not allow them to exist.
Monks, if he does not eliminate them in this way, those destructive defilements and heat of mind may arise.
If he eliminates it like that, those harmful defilements and heat of the brain will no longer exist.
Monks, these dharmas are called defilements that must be eliminated by elimination.
